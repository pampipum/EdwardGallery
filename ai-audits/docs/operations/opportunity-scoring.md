# Opportunity Scoring Rubric

## Purpose

Use this rubric to score candidate AI opportunities consistently across audits.

## Scoring Model

Score each dimension from 1 to 5.

### 1. Business Impact

Questions:

- Does this affect revenue, margin, throughput, or customer experience?
- Is the workflow important enough that improvements matter?

Guide:

- 1 = marginal impact
- 3 = meaningful team-level improvement
- 5 = major operational or commercial leverage

### 2. Time Savings

Questions:

- How much repetitive work can this remove or accelerate?
- How frequently does the workflow occur?

Guide:

- 1 = occasional low-volume task
- 3 = regular task with visible time cost
- 5 = frequent high-volume workflow

### 3. Ease of Implementation

Questions:

- Can this be implemented with existing tools and light process change?
- Does it require little engineering or procurement effort?

Guide:

- 1 = complex rollout
- 3 = moderate coordination required
- 5 = straightforward pilot possible

### 4. Risk Level

Questions:

- Would errors create compliance, legal, brand, or customer harm?
- Is human review easy to maintain?

Guide:

- 1 = high risk and low tolerance for failure
- 3 = manageable with review
- 5 = low-risk use case

### 5. Data Readiness

Questions:

- Are inputs, examples, and process steps already documented?
- Is the workflow structured enough for AI support?

Guide:

- 1 = messy or unclear inputs
- 3 = partially documented
- 5 = highly structured and documented

## Suggested Prioritization Formula

`Priority Score = Business Impact + Time Savings + Ease of Implementation + Risk Level + Data Readiness`

Maximum:

- 25

## Decision Bands

- 21 to 25 = pursue quickly
- 16 to 20 = strong candidate after validation
- 11 to 15 = keep in roadmap, not first
- 5 to 10 = low priority or poor fit

## Notes To Capture Alongside The Score

- workflow owner
- current pain description
- likely AI approach
- dependencies
- human review requirement
- pilot recommendation
