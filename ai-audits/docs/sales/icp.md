## Best Initial Segments

### Tourism and hospitality

Why it fits:

- heavy admin and communication load
- multilingual guest interactions
- seasonal staffing pressure
- many repetitive booking and coordination workflows

Examples:

- hotels
- resorts
- tourism operators
- event venues

### Boutique wealth management

Why it fits:

- strong need for discretion
- documentation-heavy work
- high value of time saved for senior staff
- preference for trusted external advisors

### Private clinics and specialist practices

Why it fits:

- high admin load outside the clinical core
- frequent scheduling and communication friction
- strong demand for careful data handling

### Manufacturing KMUs

Why it fits:

- repetitive quoting and documentation workflows
- knowledge trapped in email or long documents
- process inefficiencies that are easy to surface

## Buyer Roles

- owner or managing director
- COO or operations lead

## Buying Triggers

- pressure to improve productivity
- AI interest without a clear plan
- repeated operational bottlenecks
- staffing constraints
- concerns about privacy or compliance blocking action

## Key Objections

### "We already tested ChatGPT."

Answer:

Ad hoc use is not the same as a structured operating plan. The offer is about workflow prioritization, not random experimentation.

### "We are cautious with data."

Answer:

That caution is valid. The service explicitly limits data exposure, uses secure handling practices, and frames recommendations around Swiss data protection expectations from the start.

### "We do not want a long consulting project."

Answer:

The entry offer is fixed-scope and fast. It is designed to provide clarity before any larger commitment.
